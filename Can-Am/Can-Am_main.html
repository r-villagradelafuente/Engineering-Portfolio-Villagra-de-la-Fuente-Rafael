Heavy-Duty UTV Suspension Arm Redesign

Structural redesign of Can-Am Maverick OEM suspension arms to create a reinforced alternative for Dakar-style endurance racing, focusing on load estimation and cost-effective manufacturing.

Engineering Internship | FDC Diseño y Desarrollo | 2024 – 2025

SolidWorks Load Estimation Structural Design Design for Manufacturing

Project Overview

The main objective of this project was to take the OEM suspension arms of a Can-Am Maverick and propose a reinforced, alternative geometry suitable for extreme endurance racing, such as the Dakar Rally. The challenge was to deliver a robust structural upgrade while keeping the production process simple and cost-effective, tailored specifically for low-volume manufacturing without relying on excessively expensive processes.

Key Responsibilities & Methodology

  • Kinematic Integration: Preserved all critical hardpoints and mounting locations to the wheel hub, ensuring the new arms integrated seamlessly with the existing vehicle architecture.
  • Load Case Estimation: Calculated and applied structural load cases based on the vehicle's total weight and dynamic acceleration data provided by the client.
  • Design for Manufacturing (DFM): Developed a geometric layout that avoided high-cost, complex production methods, focusing instead on accessible fabrication techniques for low-series production.
  • CAD Development: Delivered a complete 3D CAD proposal in SolidWorks, defining the structural layout and manufacturing guidelines ready for the prototyping and physical construction phase.
