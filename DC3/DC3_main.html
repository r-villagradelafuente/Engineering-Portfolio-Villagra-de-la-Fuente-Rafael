Douglas DC-3 Flight Mechanics and Performance Analysis

Comprehensive academic evaluation detailing the aerodynamic performance, static stability, and dynamic response of the classic Douglas DC-3 aircraft.

Academic Project | Flight Mechanics Course | 2024

MATLAB Flight Mechanics Aerodynamics Stability & Control

Project Overview

The objective of this study was to model and analyze the flight behavior of the Douglas DC-3, a cornerstone of aviation history. The project involved calculating the aircraft's full flight envelope, assessing its handling qualities through static stability margins, and simulating its dynamic response to perturbations. This analysis provides a rigorous technical overview of the aircraft's operational capabilities and inherent stability characteristics.

Key Responsibilities & Methodology

  • Performance Modeling: Developed comprehensive performance charts, including power required/available, rate of climb, and range/endurance curves across various altitudes and configurations.
  • Static Stability Assessment: Calculated the longitudinal, lateral, and directional neutral points and static margins. Determined trim requirements for control surfaces under different center-of-gravity locations.
  • Dynamic Mode Characterization: Extracted and simulated the fundamental longitudinal (Phugoid, Short Period) and lateral-directional (Dutch Roll, Spiral, Roll Subsidence) flight modes.
  • Data Visualization: Utilized MATLAB to process flight mechanics equations and generate high-fidelity plots for the final technical report, ensuring clear communication of aerodynamic data.
