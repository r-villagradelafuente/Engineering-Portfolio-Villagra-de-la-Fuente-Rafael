Airfoil Lift Analysis via Discrete Vortex Method

Academic project focused on calculating the aerodynamic lift and pressure distribution of a parabolic airfoil, utilizing a panel-based numerical method programmed under potential flow theory.

Academic Project | Fluid Mechanics Course | 2023

MATLAB Computational Aerodynamics Discrete Vortex Method Potential Flow

Project Overview

The objective of this project was to calculate the aerodynamic lift and pressure distribution over a thin parabolic airfoil (5% camber) using the Discrete Vortex Method (DVM). By operating under potential flow theory, the project involved discretizing the airfoil geometry into panels and developing a robust numerical algorithm from scratch to simulate the inviscid and irrotational flow field around the body.

Key Responsibilities & Methodology

  • Algorithm Development: Programmed a complete MATLAB script to generate the airfoil geometry, calculate influence coefficient matrices, and solve the system of linear equations for vortex circulations.
  • Boundary Conditions: Correctly implemented the flow impenetrability condition and strategically placed vortices (at 25% chord) and control points (at 75% chord) to automatically satisfy the Kutta condition.
  • Model Validation: Validated the custom code by simulating a flat plate and verifying the computed circulation values and lift coefficients against analytical data from Katz & Plotkin's "Low-Speed Aerodynamics".
