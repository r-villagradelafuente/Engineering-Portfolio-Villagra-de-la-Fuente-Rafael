UTV Competition Vehicle Conceptual Design

Preliminary design and subsystem integration of a competition UTV, ensuring physical feasibility through dynamic clearance analysis and large-scale CAD management.

Engineering Internship | FDC Diseño y Desarrollo | 2024 – 2025

SolidWorks CAD Integration Conceptual Design Kinematics

Project Overview

The primary objective of this project was to develop the preliminary conceptual layout for a competition UTV. Unlike detailed component design, this phase focused heavily on vehicle packaging and systems integration—ensuring that the engine, drivetrain, suspension, and driver controls could coexist within the physical space constraints of the vehicle.

Key Responsibilities & Methodology

  • Vehicle Layout & Packaging: Led the spatial arrangement of the vehicle's primary subsystems, establishing the foundational architecture.
  • Dynamic Clearance Analysis: Simulated extreme operating conditions (maximum bump, maximum rebound, and full steering lock) to identify and resolve spatial interferences.
  • Suspension Kinematics: Analyzed the trade-offs in camber angles and geometric parameters across the full suspension stroke to optimize dynamic performance.
  • Large Assembly Management: Structured the master SolidWorks assembly, breaking down the vehicle into manageable sub-assemblies to ensure harmonic integration across a two-person engineering team.
